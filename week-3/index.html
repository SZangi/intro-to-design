To accomplish this goal we have seperated into three design sub-teams, each with a different focus area. <h3> Design Sub-Teams</h3> </br> <h4>Energy Collection </h4> The energy collection sub-team is focusing on how we want to collect energy. They began with a variety of potential energy sources and evaluated the pros and cons of each including but not limited to, energy density, ease of collection, and reliability as a source of energy. Our initial energy types included: * Geothermal * Solar (PV) * Direct Solar (Solar mirrors, solar ovens, water heaters, etc.) * Residual Heat (Compost, Sewage, etc.) * Wind * Rain After deliberating on the benefits, feasiblity, and usefulness of each, we have decided to pursue a direct solar powered energy intervention, and will be planning around using that as our energy source. The exact manner in which we will collect the energy is still to be determined and this sub-team will focus on developing that further going forwards. <h4>Energy Storage</h4> The energy storage sub-team is focusing on how the energy which is delivered by the energy capture team will be stored and delivered to the energy usage sub-team. Several ideas have been proposed which mesh well with our proposed energy capture method of direct solar, and the team will be working on narrowing down their prospects and choosing a specific idea to deliver the energy to the use sub-team in the future. Current energy storage ideas include: * Chemical Batteries * Fuel Cells * Heat reservoirs * Kinetic energy batteries * Gravitation potential energy batteries The current frontrunner in terms of being feasible to build with the limitations we have on our space while still being useful as a form of energy storage is hydrolysis fuel cells, as basic ones can be easily constructed, and they store large amounts of energy. <h4>Energy Usage & Team Management</h4> The energy usage sub-team is focused on determining how we actually use the energy which is collected by the intervention. This is the team which I am on. Based on initial brainstorming this group mainly focused on heating based intervention uses and electricity based intervention uses. Initial use ideas include: * Electric Bike * Water heaters * AC units * Building heating * Bike charger * Renewable powered lighting After the energy collection direction was cemented, we focused specifically on interventions which could easily use solar powered heat, such as solar ovens, and have been compiling various precedents for direct solar powered heating. --- Precedent websites: * <a href="https://inhabitat.com/worlds-first-molten-salt-solar-plant-produces-power-at-night/">Molten Salt Power Plant</a> * <a href="https://www.energy.gov/energysaver/solar-water-heaters">Solar Water Heater</a> * <a href="https://www.energy.gov/eere/solar/photovoltaics">Photovoltaics</a>
